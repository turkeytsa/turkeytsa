WHAT IS AUGMENTED REALITY?

Augmented reality is the ability to insert an overlay with digital and virtual information into the real world. Unlike virtual reality, which creates a totally artificial environment, augmented reality uses the existing environment and overlays new information on top of it. Augmented Reality has a lot of applications.

USES:

  • Education

    By viewing augmented models on their own devices, the students can gain a better understanding of the concepts they are studying. They can learn the details of historical events through computer generated simulations and explore the human anatomy in three dim esions. Concerns:

Augmented reality can be used for bad purposes. For example, hackers may trick users by using their location data and pull them into a place by creating fake signs. There are also privacy concerns. Face-recognition technology improves really fast and will allow us to view information about people we see like their social media profiles. Although people willingly put information online, they may not want everyone to know about their lives and background. Furthermore, there are also some psychical security concerns. People will use wearable devices like lenses, which may cause danger because of the possibility of being hacked by bad-aimed people.
