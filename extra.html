Extracurricular Activities at Istanbul Lisesi

Numune-i Terakki

Numune-i Terakki is Istanbul Lisesi’s seasonally published science magazine prepared by students and alumni. First published in 1887, it was the first student magazine in Ottoman Empire. Meaning “instance of development” originally, Numune-i Terakki is also the very first name of our school. Thanks to the founder of our school, mathematician Mehmet Nadir Bey, the magazine had a very good academic level from the beginning, and at that time the articles and problems that were published were interesting for both primary and high school students. The publication stopped in 1888, but in 2009 students took action to publish the magazine again. In June 2010, they managed to bring Numune-i Terakki back to publication again. Numune-i Terakki is also the name of the Science Week comitee of Istanbul Lisesi. Every year in January, the comitee arranges a week-long science and technology event for both students of Istanbul Lisesi and students from other high schools. They also organize trivia contests about biology, maths, chemistry and physics. Experiment displays are also a part of science week. Visitors can make experiments under the supervision of comitee members.

International Chess Festival

Being the first international chess festival for high school level in Turkey, the International Chess Festival is held every year since 2001. Nearly 60 schools from both Turkey and other countries take part in this festival which is arranged by the Chess Club. Aside from chess, other strategy games such as checkers and shogi are played as well. The 17th Chesstival took place in May this year.

Science Olympiad

Students interested in science have the chance to participate in the Science Olympiad organized by the Scientific and Technological Research Council of Turkey (TUBITAK). This way, TUBITAK can identify scientifically gifted children and encourage them to develop their talents. The National Science Olympiad has been organized since 1993 in 5 subjects: math, biology, chemistry, physics and computer science. Istanbul Lisesi takes part in the math, biology and computer olympiad competitions and has achieved great successes.

Sportfest

The first and biggest sports festivall in Turkey is organised in Istanbul Lisesi every year. Within the so called Sportfest, basketball, football and volleyball tournaments with participants from different high schools are organised, as well as lots of workshops of the professionals. Throughout the fest, 5030 students are usually hosted in total.

Culture Week

At the last week of every school year Culture week is organised by the students of the Culture Comitee. Throughout this week students have a chance to participate in tournaments, workshops and have converstaions with famous people like authors, musicians and poets.
