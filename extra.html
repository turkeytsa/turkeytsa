Extracurricular Scientific Activities at Istanbul Lisesi

Numune-i Terakki

Numune-i Terakki is Istanbul Lisesi’s seasonal published science magazine prepared by the students and alumni. First published in 1887, it was the first student magazine in Ottoman Empire. Meaning “instance of development” originally, Numune-i Terakki is also the very first name of our school. Thanks to the founder of our school, mathematician Mehmet Nadir Bey, the magazine had a very good academic level from the beginning, and at that time articles and problems that were interesting for both primary and high school students were published. The publication stopped in 1888, but in 2009 students took steps to publish the magazine again. In June 2010, they managed to bring Numune-i Terakki to its publication life again. Numune-i Terakki is also the name of the science comitee of Istanbul Lisesi. Every year in January, the comitee arranges a week for both students of Istanbul Lisesi and students from other high schools, which are interested in science and technology. They also organize knowledge contest about biology, maths, chemistry and physics. Experiment exhibitons are also a part of science week. Visitors can make experiments with members of the comitee.

International Chess Festival

Being the first international chess festival for high school level in Turkey, the International Chess Festival is held every year since 2001. Nearly 60 schools from both Turkey and other countries take part in this festival which is arranged by the chess club of the school. Conferences about chess are held and students, especially students attending chess competitions, play chess and discuss about the techniques. The chess comitee bring every year brain teaser games out of chess, like dama and shogi.This year,the 17th chess festival took place and they also arranged competitions.

TUBITAK Clubs

Students interested in science have the chance to participate in Science Olympics organized by the Scientific and Technological Research Council of Turkey (TUBITAK). This way, TUBITAK can identify scientifically gifted children and to encourage them to develop their talents. The National Science Olympics has been organized since 1993 in 3 subjects: maths,biology, chemistry, physics and computer science. Istanbul Lisesi attend maths, biology and computer science and has achieved great succes. Lessons for oliympics from professional teachers are given from the preparation year of the high school regularly.
